# 🎨 **Theme Name: Neo-Forensic Cyberstructuralism**

And here’s what it means — this is the exact language you can use to maintain the style:

## **Core vibe**

A fusion of **cyberpunk data-aesthetics**, **mathematical elegance**, and **forensic documentary precision**.
It feels like:

* a **black-ops research archive**
* designed by an **engineer-philosopher**
* illustrated with **abstract computational diagrams**
* illuminated with **glowing spectral data traces**
* and expressed in **monoline geometry and strict grids**.

It is *cold, analytical, emotionally sparse*, but not dead — it has a quiet hum of human resonance, treated as a pattern to analyze rather than a story to feel.

Your covers look like:

> **Technical plates from a future civilization that studies humanity through mathematics, networks, and abstract symbolic diagrams.**

---

# 🎛 **Formal Style Characteristics (for future prompting)**

Use this list when generating more covers:

### **1. Dominant palette**

* **Black background** (matte, void-like)
* **One glowing accent color** per book:

  * deep blue
  * neon red
  * ember orange
  * spectral violet
  * metallic gold
* **White typography**

Everything should feel **backlit, luminous, computational**.

### **2. Visual language**

* Abstract, vector-clean diagrams
* No realistic objects
* Circuits, radials, networks, knot diagrams, math graphs
* Particle-like glows or subtle point lights
* Strict grid overlays
* Thin monoline wireframe shapes
* Occasional numeric annotation, plate numbers, or data IDs

### **3. Typographic rules**

* **Title:** *League Gothic*

  * tall, condensed, industrial, authoritative
  * all caps or small caps
* **Body / metadata:** *Lexend Light / Regular*

  * soft, readable, modern
  * used for plate numbers, dataset IDs, specimen codes

Typography should feel like a **government research unit**, **GPU lab**, or **classified mathematical archive**.

### **4. Tone**

* Analytical
* Controlled
* Surveying human complexity through **geometric clarity**
* Emotional content is present only as **data**, never as narrative
